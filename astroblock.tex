\begin{abstract}

An interoperability  development driver based on (Open) Blockchain ledger technology is considered for Astronomy \& Astrophysics Education, Research and Development from K-12 to tertiary-research level or more simply put for Astronomy Open Development (AOD). AOD is defined as a complimentary system of funding \& development through open governance of existing Astronomical institutions. Funding is proposed to be provided at least initially from the decentralized finance movement (DEFI) and by extension the Decentralized Science Movement (DESCI) with involvement by vetted astronomical institutions with the assumption that DEFI \& DESCI are key ideas of the open Blockchain stack that provide solutions when limitations in conventional funding may stifle development or fail it. Three salient use-cases are selected and examined: (i) Data-Tsunami ETL Optimization: including Velocity, Volume, Veracity and Variety of Astronomical data streams through Telescope broker Federated Learning incentive Blockchain models (ii) Conserving plus value-adding of Astronomical Observatory patrimony via Blockchain primitives like NFTs; (iii) Mitigating the financial impact of debasement of fiat currencies that impact budgets for development and to create a more even `playing field' via collaborations, liquidity pools and other Blockchain tooling solutions.  An important corollary arises: Could AOD limit the so-called the "Brain Drain" or steep decline in the half-life function of PhD Astronomy majors in US public universities as well as others? In this white paper we assume the Nakamoto Consensus and re-examine and attempt to extend the seminal works of Kardashev and Sagan. We point out a potential flaw of the Kardeshev relation and normalize it on the bitcoin hashrate. The Bitcoin hash-rate is taken as a proxy of the information transaction richness of a civilization. In this analysis a distinction is required between Energy Consumption and Energy Production of a civilization. Finally, an attempt is made to identify elements for a path to Open development that includes on-boarding, discussion of community metrics in 'tooling tokenomics' and adoption of existing governance protocols where the role of prediction markets for AOD is discussed, proposed for consideration by international astronomical community bodies like the IAU, IVOA, Public Universities, etc via public metrics. This paper is an attempt to kick-start this discussion in earnest to achieve a value-added programmable incentive layer upon open Blockchain protocol standards for the advancement of Astronomy.

\label{subsec: review}

\cite{arvindandclark2017} show insurmountable evidence as do other authors therein that standard open Blockchain protocols are much rooted in academic studies of cryptography.
